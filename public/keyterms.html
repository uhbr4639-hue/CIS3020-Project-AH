Key Terms (Alphabetical)






Asset

An asset is anything that holds current or future economic value that any entity (person, company, etc.) owns.

Capital Gain

A capital gain is the profit recieved from the sale of asset(s) or from investments.

Cash Flow

Cash flow refers to the overall amount of money that "flows" into and out of a business or project.

Dividend

Dividends are the amount of money that a company pays its shareholders based on a portion of its profits.

Equity

Equity is the value of an asset after subtracting any potential liabilities from it. In other terms, Asset Value - Liabilities = Equity.

Liability

A liability is any sort of debt or financial obligation an entity is expected to pay another party.

Portfolio

In finance, a portfolio is a collection of investments made by an entity, usually with certain, set financial goals.

Shares

Shares are units of a company's stock that are owned by shareholders and can be quantified.

Shareholder

Shareholders are people who owns shares of a company and are likely recieving various benefits, depending on the arrangement.

Stock

Stocks are essentially a partial ownership of a company's assets and future income that can be bought by investors. This is usually done by investors in order to sell the stocks at a higher price than they were bought, assuming that the company has increased in value, but also to recieve dividends.
